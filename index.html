Pedro Pinheiro-Chagas

I am a postdoctoral scholar working with Stanislas Dehaene at NeuroSpin, in the outskirts of Paris.

In March 2018, I will join Josef Parvizi at the Human Intracranial Cognitive Electrophysiology Program of Stanford University.

My research focuses on understanding the architecture and dynamics of human high-level cognition, with particular interests in elementary mathematics and compositionality.

I use a combination of continuous behavioral measures (trajectory-tracking) and machine learning applied to time-resolved brain signals, such as MEG and intracranial EEG.
